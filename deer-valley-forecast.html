Day 1 — Friday
rain → freezing rain/sleet as temps slide through 30s
Risk: HIGH

Weather

Wet early, then a transition window where precipitation changes type as temperatures drop. Expect the “it was fine 2 hours ago” trap.

Roads

Pavement starts wet, then flash-freeze after sunset. Bridges, low dips, shaded curves ice first. Rural roads typically get zero help.

Day 2 — Friday Night
freezing rain + sleet; temps in the 20s
Risk: VERY HIGH

Weather

This is the classic icing phase: liquid/partials freezing on contact and sleet compacting into a base layer.

Roads

Worst traction phase. Short steep inclines near creek cuts become “spin and slide.” Stopping mid-hill can become a one-way commitment.

Day 3 — Saturday
sleet / snow showers / freezing rain mix; windy
Risk: VERY HIGH

Weather

Cold enough for snow, but often still mixed. Wind adds drift and visibility issues on exposed segments.

Roads

No meaningful melt. Snow may hide the ice layer. Downhill braking = slide. Uphill traction = unreliable even with AWD.

Day 4 — Saturday Night
clearing trend; temps crash to single digits/teens
Risk: EXTREME

Weather

Precip tapers, but the temperature drop hardens everything. Any wet surface becomes a skating rink.

Roads

Everything freezes solid. Ruts become ice rails. Any earlier melt refreezes invisibly. This is the “quietly lethal” window.

Day 5 — Sunday
mostly sunny; still very cold
Risk: HIGH

Weather

Sun returns, but air stays cold. No new precip doesn’t mean “safe.”

Roads

Ridge tops may improve slightly in sun. Shaded valleys and north-facing slopes remain icy all day.

Day 6 — Monday

Weather

Partial daytime thaw in the sun, with overnight refreeze. The freeze/thaw cycle manufactures black ice.

Roads

Day melt → night refreeze. Morning travel is the most dangerous. Expect slick spots to migrate as water runs into dips.

Day 7 — Tuesday

Weather

Meaningful thaw begins. Still watch overnight lows hovering near freezing.

Roads

Terrain & Decision Aids Deer Valley / Tucker

Assumptions

  • Rural temp runs ~2–5°F colder than town at night.
  • No reliable road pretreatment on side roads.
  • Short steep grades exist near creek cuts and draws.
  • Main hazard: ice > snow depth.

Highest-Risk Spots

  • Creek crossings & low dips (cold air pools).
  • Short steep climbs after low points.
  • Tree-covered curves (shade = ice persistence).
  • Bridge decks & culverts (freeze first).

Go / No-Go Rules (Quick)

  • NO-GO if freezing rain is falling or just ended after sunset.
  • NO-GO if you must stop mid-incline (traffic) on Tucker/steep hills.
  • CAUTION if daytime melt occurred and temps drop below 32°F at night.
  • BEST WINDOW is midday sun on Sunday/Monday only if temps rise and stay up.

Prep Checklist

  • Fuel up early; assume limited travel later.
  • Charge devices + power banks; keep flashlights handy.
  • Ice risk: plan for downed branches/lines on rural roads.
  • Stock 2–3 days of basics (food, meds, pet needs).
  • Keep a traction plan: salt/sand kitty litter, shovel, tow strap.
